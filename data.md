# Juz 1

## Page 2 (2:1–5)
-   The opening of **Surah Al-Baqarah**, beginning with the detached letters _Alif-Lam-Mim_.
-   The Qur’an is established as a Book of guidance with no doubt in it for the God-conscious.
-   Definition of the righteous: those who believe in the Unseen and establish prayer.
-   They spend from what Allah has provided and believe in all revealed scriptures.
-   These are the people who are truly successful and on the path of guidance.


## Page 3 (2:6–16)
-   Description of the disbelievers: those whose hearts and hearing are sealed due to their rejection.
-   Introduction of the **Hypocrites**: those who say they believe but do not.
-   They try to deceive Allah and the believers, but they only deceive themselves.
-   When told not to cause corruption on earth, they claim to be "peacemakers."
-   They are likened to those who trade guidance for error, ending in a profitless bargain.


## Page 4 (2:17–24)
-   Parable of the hypocrites: like those who light a fire but are left in darkness when it goes out.
-   Parable of the rainstorm: their state of confusion and fear in the face of truth.
-   A call to all mankind to worship the Lord who created them and the heavens/earth.
-   Challenge to the doubters: produce a single Surah like the Qur'an if you are truthful.
-   Warning of the Fire whose fuel is men and stones, prepared for the disbelievers.


## Page 5 (2:25–29)
-   Glad tidings for believers: Gardens with flowing rivers and pure spouses.
-   Allah does not shy away from using the parable of a gnat or something smaller.
-   The believers recognize the truth in parables, while the disbelievers are further confused.
-   Questioning mankind: How can you disbelieve when Allah gave you life from nothing?
-   Reaffirming that to Allah belongs everything in the heavens and the earth.


## Page 6 (2:30–37)
-   The story of the creation of **Adam** and Allah's announcement to the angels.
-   The angels question the creation of man, but Allah affirms His supreme knowledge.
-   Allah teaches Adam the names of all things, proving his potential to the angels.
-   The command to prostrate to Adam; **Iblis** refuses out of arrogance and becomes a disbeliever.
-   Adam and Hawwa (Eve) are settled in the Garden, tempted by Satan, and then sent to earth after repenting.


## Page 7 (2:38–48)
-   Allah promises that guidance will come to mankind; those who follow it shall have no fear.
-   The address to the **Children of Israel**: a call to remember Allah’s favors and fulfill their covenant.
-   The command to believe in the Qur’an which confirms their own previous scriptures.
-   Warning against "selling" Allah’s verses for a small price or mixing truth with falsehood.
-   Reminder of the Day of Judgment where no soul will avail another and no intercession will be accepted.


## Page 8 (2:49–57)
-   Recalling the rescue from Pharaoh, who slaughtered the sons and spared the women.
-   The miracle of the parting of the sea and the drowning of Pharaoh’s army.
-   The appointment of 40 nights for Musa and the people's mistake of worshipping the calf.
-   The Israelites' demand to see Allah "plainly," resulting in them being struck by a thunderbolt.
-   Mention of the Manna and Salwa (food) and the clouds provided for shade in the desert.


## Page 9 (2:58–61)
-   The command to enter the city with humility and say "Hittatun" (Forgive us).
-   The miracle of the twelve springs gushing from the rock when Musa struck it with his staff.
-   The people’s dissatisfaction with heavenly food, asking instead for "herbs, cucumbers, and lentils."
-   Musa rebukes them for wanting to exchange what is better for what is inferior.
-   Their rebellion and killing of Prophets resulted in humiliation and the wrath of Allah.


## Page 10 (2:62–69)
-   Affirmation that believers, Jews, Christians, and Sabians who believe and do good will have their reward.
-   Recalling the covenant made under Mount Tur when the mountain was raised over them.
-   The story of the **Sabbath-breakers** who were turned into despised apes.
-   The beginning of the story of the **Cow**: Allah commands the people to sacrifice a cow.
-   The people begin to ask unnecessary, stalling questions about the cow's color and age.


## Page 11 (2:70–76)
-   The final description of the cow: a bright yellow heifer, unyoked and without blemish.
-   They finally sacrifice the cow, though they were near to refusing the command.
-   The miracle of using a part of the cow to bring a dead man to life to identify his killer.
-   Warning that their hearts became harder than rocks, as some rocks at least fear Allah.
-   Exposure of the hypocrites among them who pretend to believe when meeting the Muslims.


## Page 12 (2:77–83)
-   Allah knows what they conceal and what they reveal.
-   Criticism of the "unlettered" among them who follow only wishful thinking and hearsay.
-   Woe to those who write the "scripture" with their own hands and claim it is from Allah.
-   Refutation of the claim that the Fire will only touch them for a few days.
-   The covenant: Worship only Allah, be kind to parents, orphans, and the needy, and establish prayer.


## Page 13 (2:84–88)
-   The covenant to not shed each other’s blood or evict one another from their homes.
-   Their contradictory behavior: they evicted their own people but then tried to ransom them.
-   Belief in parts of the Scripture while rejecting others leads to disgrace in this life.
-   Mention of the support given to Prophet 'Isa (Jesus) via the Holy Spirit.
-   Their arrogant claim: "Our hearts are wrapped (sealed against your message)."


## Page 14 (2:89–93)
-   They rejected the Qur'an despite knowing it was the truth they had been waiting for.
-   They "sold their souls" by rejecting what Allah revealed out of envy for the Prophet.
-   When told to believe in the Qur'an, they say "We believe only in what was sent to us."
-   Rebuttal: If you believed your own book, why did you kill the Prophets of old?
-   Their hearts were filled with the love of the calf because of their disbelief.


## Page 15 (2:94–101)
-   If the Home of the Hereafter is for you alone, then "long for death" if you are truthful.
-   They will never long for death because of the evil deeds their hands have sent forward.
-   You will find them the "greediest of people for life," even more than the polytheists.
-   Declaration of enmity toward **Jibril** (Gabriel) is enmity toward Allah, as he brings revelation.
-   The pattern of the People of the Book throwing Allah’s covenant behind their backs.


## Page 16 (2:102–105)
-   The story of **Sulaiman** (Solomon) and the refutation that he practiced magic.
-   Magic was taught by devils and the two angels **Harut and Marut** as a trial.
-   People learned magic to separate man and wife, but they cannot harm anyone except by Allah's leave.
-   Magic is a losing trade that has no share of good in the Hereafter.
-   Warning to the believers to use respectful language (_Unzurna_) rather than ambiguous words (_Ra'ina_).


## Page 17 (2:106–112)
-   The concept of **Naskh** (Abrogation): Allah replaces a verse with one better or similar.
-   Do you want to question your Messenger as Musa was questioned before?
-   Many of the People of the Book wish to turn the believers back to disbelief out of envy.
-   Refutation of the claim that only Jews or Christians will enter Paradise.
-   True success is for whoever submits their face to Allah and is a doer of good.


## Page 18 (2:113–119)
-   Jews and Christians criticize each other’s validity despite both reading Scripture.
-   Who is more unjust than those who prevent Allah’s name from being mentioned in His mosques?
-   **"To Allah belongs the East and the West"**—wherever you turn, there is the Face of Allah.
-   Refutation of the claim that "Allah has taken a son"; He is exalted above such needs.
-   The Prophet is sent with the truth as a bearer of glad tidings and a warner.


## Page 19 (2:120–126)
-   The Jews and Christians will never be satisfied with you until you follow their way.
-   Those given the Book who recite it properly are the true believers in it.
-   Recalling the favor upon the Children of Israel and the warning of the Day of Judgment.
-   The testing of **Ibrahim** with commands which he fulfilled, leading to his leadership.
-   Ibrahim’s prayer for Makkah to be a city of peace and for his offspring to be believers.


## Page 20 (2:127–134)
-   Ibrahim and Ismail raising the foundations of the **Kaaba** and praying for its acceptance.
-   Ibrahim’s prayer for a Messenger to be sent from among his descendants to teach them.
-   Only a fool would turn away from the religion of Ibrahim.
-   Ibrahim’s legacy to his sons and Yaqub’s (Jacob) legacy to his sons: "Die not except as Muslims."
-   The declaration: "That was a nation that has passed; for it is what it earned, and for you is what you earn."


## Page 21 (2:135–141)
-   Rejection of the call to "become Jews or Christians" to be guided; follow the way of Ibrahim.
-   The creed of Islam: Belief in Allah, the Qur'an, and all Prophets without distinction.
-   If they believe as you believe, they are guided; if they turn away, they are in dissension.
-   The "Baptism of Allah" (_Sibghat Allah_)—who is better than Allah in baptism?
-   Rebuttal to the claim that Ibrahim, Ismail, Ishaq, and Yaqub were Jews or Christians.

# Juz 2

## Page 22 (2:142–145)
-   The change of the **Qiblah** (direction of prayer) from Jerusalem to the Kaaba.
-   The foolish will ask why the direction changed; say: "To Allah belongs the East and the West."
-   This change was a test to distinguish those who follow the Prophet from those who turn back.
-   The Prophet's longing to face the Kaaba is fulfilled by Allah’s command.
-   Even if the People of the Book were shown every sign, they would not follow your Qiblah.


## Page 23 (2:146–153)
-   The People of the Book recognize the truth as they recognize their own sons.
-   Do not be among the doubters; everyone has a direction, so "race toward all that is good."
-   The Prophet’s mission: to recite verses, purify the people, and teach the Book and Wisdom.
-   "Remember Me; I will remember you. Be grateful to Me and do not deny Me."
-   Seek help through **Patience and Prayer**; indeed, Allah is with the patient.


## Page 154 (2:154–163)
-   Do not say that those killed in the way of Allah are dead; they are alive, but you perceive it not.
-   Believers will be tested with fear, hunger, and loss of wealth and lives.
-   The response of the patient: "To Allah we belong, and to Him we shall return."
-   The rituals of **Safa and Marwa** are among the symbols of Allah.
-   Warning to those who hide the clear proofs and guidance Allah has revealed.


## Page 25 (2:164–169)
-   Signs of Allah in creation: the heavens, earth, night, day, ships, rain, and winds.
-   Criticism of those who take "equals" to Allah and love them as they should love Allah.
-   On the Day of Judgment, the leaders will disassociate themselves from their followers.
-   Follow what is lawful and good on earth; do not follow the footsteps of **Satan**.
-   Satan only commands you to evil and immorality and to say about Allah what you do not know.


## Page 26 (2:170–176)
-   Criticism of blind following of ancestors: "Even if their fathers understood nothing?"
-   Parable of the disbelievers: like cattle that hear only the sound of a shout.
-   Prohibition of carrion, blood, swine, and meat dedicated to other than Allah.
-   Necessity allows for exceptions if one is forced by hunger without desire for sin.
-   Those who hide the Book for worldly gain eat nothing but "fire" in their bellies.


## Page 27 (2:177–181)
-   The **"Verse of Birr"** (Piety): faith is not just turning faces East or West.
-   Piety includes giving wealth to kin and orphans, keeping covenants, and being patient in hardship.
-   Laws of **Qisas** (Retribution) are established to save lives and ensure justice.
-   The obligation of making a **Will** (bequest) for parents and relatives before death.
-   Severe warning against anyone who alters a will after hearing it.


## Page 28 (2:182–186)
-   One who mediates a fair adjustment to a will is not a sinner.
-   **Fasting** is prescribed as it was for those before you, so that you may become God-conscious.
-   Exceptions for the sick and travelers; the month of **Ramadan** is when the Qur'an was revealed.
-   Allah desires ease for you, not hardship.
-   "When My servants ask you about Me, indeed I am near... I respond to the caller."


## Page 29 (2:187–190)
-   Marital relations are permitted during the nights of the fast.
-   The boundary for eating and drinking: until the white thread of dawn becomes distinct.
-   Do not consume each other’s wealth unjustly or bribe judges.
-   Rulings on the "New Moons" as signs to mark time for people and for Hajj.
-   Fight in the way of Allah those who fight you, but do not transgress.


## Page 30 (2:191–196)
-   Kill the aggressors wherever you find them and expel them from where they expelled you.
-   Fighting is prohibited near the Sacred Mosque unless they attack you there.
-   Fight until there is no more persecution and the religion is for Allah.
-   Spending in the way of Allah: "Do not throw yourselves into destruction with your own hands."
-   Detailed rules for completing **Hajj and Umrah**, including penalties for those who cannot finish.


## Page 31 (2:197–202)
-   Hajj is in well-known months; no foul speech or arguing during the pilgrimage.
-   "Take provisions, but indeed, the best provision is **Taqwa** (piety)."
-   It is no sin to seek bounty (trade) from your Lord during Hajj.
-   Contrast between two types of prayers: those who ask only for this world and those who ask for both.
-   "Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the Fire."


## Page 32 (2:203–210)
-   Remembrance of Allah during the "Appointed Days" (Tashreeq).
-   Description of the smooth-talking hypocrite whose actions are destructive.
-   Description of the believer who sells his soul to seek the pleasure of Allah.
-   O you who believe, enter into **Islam completely** and follow not Satan.
-   Warning of the "Slippery Slope": Do not deviate after clear proofs have come.


## Page 33 (2:211–215)
-   Ask the Children of Israel how many clear signs they were given.
-   Worldly life is made alluring to disbelievers, who mock those who believe.
-   Mankind was once one nation; then Allah sent Prophets to judge between them.
-   Belief in the face of trials: "When will the help of Allah come? Unquestionably, it is near."
-   Guidance on charity: spend on parents, relatives, orphans, the needy, and travelers.


## Page 34 (2:216–219)
-   Fighting is prescribed for you, though you may dislike it.
-   "Perhaps you dislike a thing which is good for you and like a thing which is bad for you."
-   Rulings on fighting during the **Sacred Months**; hindering people from Allah’s path is a greater sin.
-   Those who believe and emigrate (Hijrah) and strive have hope in Allah’s mercy.
-   The prohibition of **Intoxicants and Gambling**; they contain some benefit but their sin is greater.


## Page 35 (2:220–224)
-   Questions regarding the orphans: "To improve their lot is best."
-   Prohibition of marrying polytheist men or women until they believe.
-   A believing slave is better than a wealthy polytheist who attracts you.
-   Rulings on menstruation: it is a discomfort, so stay away from wives until they are pure.
-   Warning against using oaths in Allah’s name as an excuse to avoid doing good.


## Page 36 (2:225–230)
-   Allah does not hold you for "unintentional" oaths, but for what your hearts intend.
-   Rulings on **Ila** (taking an oath to abstain from one's wife).
-   Divorced women must wait three menstrual cycles before remarrying.
-   Divorce is twice; after that, either keep her with kindness or release her with grace.
-   The "Three-Talaq" rule: a woman cannot remarry her first husband until she has married and divorced another.


## Page 37 (2:231–233)
-   When the waiting period is nearly over, either retain wives in kindness or let them go.
-   Do not retain them just to hurt them; do not take Allah's verses in mockery.
-   Do not prevent divorced women from remarrying their husbands if they agree.
-   Rulings on **Breastfeeding**: mothers should suckle for two full years; the father provides for them.
-   No soul is burdened with more than it can bear regarding the care of children.


## Page 38 (2:234–237)
-   The waiting period for widows is **four months and ten days**.
-   There is no blame in hinting at a marriage proposal during this waiting period.
-   Do not conclude the marriage contract until the prescribed term is reached.
-   Rulings on divorce before consummation: half the dowry is due unless waived.
-   The importance of "not forgetting to do good between yourselves."


## Page 39 (2:238–245)
-   Guard strictly the five daily prayers, especially the **Middle Prayer** (Asr).
-   If you are in fear (war/danger), pray while walking or riding.
-   Provision for widows: a year's maintenance and not being evicted (later modified by inheritance laws).
-   Reflect on the thousands who fled their homes for fear of death and were then brought back to life.
-   "Who will lend Allah a **Goodly Loan** (charity) so He may multiply it for him?"


## Page 40 (2:246–248)
-   The story of the Israelites asking their Prophet for a King to lead them in battle.
-   Their initial hesitation when the command to fight actually came.
-   The appointment of **Talut** (Saul) as King, despite the people's objection to his lack of wealth.
-   His sign of kingship: the return of the **Ark of the Covenant** carried by angels.
-   Reassurance that Allah grants kingship to whom He wills and He is All-Encompassing.


## Page 41 (2:249–252)
-   Talut tests his army at the river: those who drink more than a handful are disqualified.
-   A small, faithful group remains to face the giant **Jalut** (Goliath) and his army.
-   The believers' prayer: "Our Lord, pour upon us patience and plant our feet firmly."
-   **Dawud** (David) kills Jalut; Allah grants Dawud kingship and wisdom.
-   "If Allah did not check one set of people by means of another, the earth would be full of mischief."

# Juz 4

## Page 62 (3:93–100)
-   All food was lawful for the Children of Israel except what Israel prohibited for himself.
-   The command to follow the religion of Ibrahim, who was a pure monotheist.
-   The **Kaaba** in Makkah is the first House of Worship for mankind and a source of guidance.
-   Hajj to the House is a duty for those who are able to perform it.
-   A warning to the People of the Book for rejecting signs and hindering others from the path.


## Page 63 (3:101–108)
-   Fear Allah as He deserves to be feared and die only in a state of Islam.
-   Hold fast to the **"Rope of Allah"** (the Qur'an) and do not be divided.
-   Remember Allah’s favor in uniting your hearts when you were enemies on the brink of a pit of fire.
-   Let there be a community among you that invites to good and forbids evil.
-   Do not be like those who divided into sects after clear proofs came to them.


## Page 64 (3:109–115)
-   Everything in the heavens and earth belongs to Allah, and all matters return to Him.
-   You are the **"Best Nation"** produced for mankind because you enjoin good and forbid evil.
-   If the People of the Book had believed, it would have been better for them.
-   They will not harm you except for a slight annoyance; if they fight you, they will show their backs.
-   Not all of them are the same; some recite Allah's verses and prostrate at night.


## Page 65 (3:116–121)
-   The wealth and children of disbelievers will not benefit them against Allah.
-   Their charity is compared to a freezing wind that destroys the harvest of an oppressive people.
-   O you who believe, do not take outsiders as intimate friends; they desire your ruin.
-   Their hatred is evident in their speech, but what their hearts hide is even greater.
-   Recalling when the Prophet left his family to post the believers for the **Battle of Uhud**.


## Page 66 (3:122–132)
-   Allah comforted the two groups who were about to lose heart, for He is their Protector.
-   A reminder of **Badr**, where Allah gave victory despite the believers being weak.
-   Allah’s promise to help with thousands of angels if the believers are patient and God-conscious.
-   Prohibition of **Riba** (Usury), especially when doubled and multiplied.
-   Obey Allah and the Messenger so that you may receive mercy.


## Page 67 (3:133–140)
-   Race toward forgiveness and a Paradise as vast as the heavens and earth.
-   Qualities of the righteous: spending in ease/hardship, restraining anger, and forgiving people.
-   If they commit a sin, they remember Allah and seek immediate forgiveness.
-   Travel through the earth and see the end of those who denied the truth.
-   "Do not weaken or grieve; you will be superior if you are true believers."


## Page 68 (3:141–148)
-   Trials are sent to "sift" the believers and destroy the disbelievers.
-   Did you think you would enter Paradise without being tested for your patience and struggle?
-   You used to long for death (martyrdom), and now you have seen it before your eyes.
-   Muhammad is only a Messenger; if he dies, will you turn back on your heels?
-   No soul dies except by Allah's leave at an appointed time.


## Page 69 (3:149–153)
-   If you obey the disbelievers, they will turn you back and you will be losers.
-   Allah is your true Protector and the best of Helpers.
-   Fear is cast into the hearts of the disbelievers because of their Shirk.
-   Analysis of the setback at Uhud: it happened after you lost courage and disagreed.
-   Allah forgave you, for He is the possessor of bounty over the believers.


## Page 70 (3:154–157)
-   After the distress, Allah sent security in the form of slumber upon the true believers.
-   The hypocrites were worried only about themselves, harboring ignorant thoughts about Allah.
-   Say: "Even if you were in your houses, those decreed to be killed would have gone to their death."
-   This was a test of what is in your chests and to purify your hearts.
-   If you are killed in the way of Allah, His forgiveness and mercy are better than any wealth.


## Page 71 (3:158–165)
-   Whether you die or are killed, you will be gathered back to Allah.
-   The Prophet’s **gentleness** is a mercy from Allah; had he been harsh, people would have left him.
-   Consult with the believers and, once a decision is made, put your trust in Allah.
-   "If Allah helps you, none can overcome you; if He forsakes you, who can help?"
-   It is not for any Prophet to act dishonestly with the spoils of war.


## Page 72 (3:166–173)
-   The events of Uhud were to distinguish the true believers from the hypocrites.
-   The hypocrites said they would have followed the Prophet if they "knew how to fight."
-   Do not think of those killed in Allah’s way as dead; they are alive and provided for.
-   They rejoice in what Allah has given them and for those they left behind.
-   Praise for the believers who were undeterred by the news of an approaching enemy army.


## Page 73 (3:174–180)
-   The believers returned from the encounter with favor and grace, seeking only Allah’s pleasure.
-   Satan only frightens his allies; "Fear Me, if you are true believers."
-   Do not let those who hasten into disbelief grieve you; they harm Allah not at all.
-   Disbelievers should not think that the respite given to them is good; it only increases their sin.
-   Warning to the stingy: the wealth they withhold will be a "collar" around their necks on Judgment Day.


## Page 74 (3:181–186)
-   Allah heard those who mockingly said, "Allah is poor and we are rich."
-   Their claim that a Prophet must bring a fire-sacrifice is countered by their killing of previous Prophets.
-   **"Every soul shall taste death."**
-   The life of this world is nothing but a deceptive enjoyment.
-   You will surely be tested in your wealth and yourselves and will hear hurtful words from others.


## Page 75 (3:187–194)
-   The covenant was taken from the People of the Book to make the Scripture known, not hide it.
-   Condemnation of those who love to be praised for deeds they did not do.
-   Reflection on the creation of the heavens and earth as signs for the "People of Understanding."
-   The prayer of the believers: "Our Lord, You did not create this in vain; protect us from the Fire."
-   A plea for forgiveness and to be gathered with the righteous after death.


## Page 76 (3:195–200)
-   Allah responds: "I never lose the work of any worker among you, male or female."
-   Those who emigrated, were expelled, and fought for Allah will have their sins expiated.
-   Do not be deceived by the temporary prosperity of the disbelievers.
-   Some among the People of the Book are humble and believe in the Qur'an.
-   The Surah ends with the command: "Be patient, excel in patience, and remain stationed (ready)."


## Page 77 (4:1–6)
-   The beginning of **Surah An-Nisa**: Mankind was created from a single soul.
-   Command to give orphans their property and not to exchange their good for your bad.
-   Permission to marry up to four women, but only if you can maintain absolute justice.
-   The obligation to give women their **Mahr** (dowry) as a gift.
-   Do not give wealth to the "weak-minded"; provide for them but manage the property until they are mature.


## Page 78 (4:7–11)
-   Men and women both have a defined share in inheritance from parents and kin.
-   Speak kindly to relatives, orphans, and the needy who are present during the distribution.
-   Warning: Consuming the property of orphans unjustly is like eating fire.
-   General inheritance rule: A male’s share is equal to that of two females.
-   Specific shares for parents are defined based on whether the deceased had children.


## Page 79 (4:12–14)
-   Inheritance shares for husbands and wives from each other’s estates.
-   Rules for **Kalalah** (those who leave no parents or children), involving siblings.
-   These distributions happen only after paying debts and fulfilling bequests (wills).
-   These laws are the **"Limits of Allah"** (Hudud Allah).
-   Success for those who obey these limits; a humiliating punishment for those who transgress.


## Page 80 (4:15–19)
-   Early rulings on "illegal lewdness" and the door to repentance.
-   Repentance is for those who sin in ignorance and repent quickly; not for those who wait until death.
-   Prohibition of "inheriting" women against their will or treating them harshly to take their dowry.
-   **"Live with them (wives) in kindness."**
-   Even if you dislike a quality in your wife, perhaps Allah has placed much good in it.


## Page 81 (4:20–23)
-   If you wish to replace one wife with another, do not take back any part of the dowry.
-   Marriage to women who were married to one’s father is strictly prohibited.
-   A detailed list of forbidden marriages: mothers, daughters, sisters, aunts, and nieces.
-   Prohibition of marrying foster-mothers, foster-sisters, and two sisters at the same time.
-   Allah is Oft-Forgiving and Most Merciful.




# Juz 5

## Page 82 (4:24–26)
-   Prohibition of marrying women already in a marriage contract, with specific exceptions.
-   All other women are lawful for marriage provided the intent is a legal bond, not lust.
-   Reiteration of the obligation to pay the dowry (Mahr) as a legal duty.
-   Permission to marry from among the believing maidservants if one cannot afford free women.
-   Allah’s desire to make things clear and lead the believers toward the righteous paths of the past.


## Page 83 (4:27–33)
-   Allah wishes to turn to you in mercy, while those following lusts want you to go astray.
-   The human being is created weak, so Allah seeks to lighten the legal burdens.
-   Prohibition of consuming each other's wealth unjustly; trade must be based on mutual consent.
-   The command to avoid "Major Sins"; doing so will cause Allah to expiate minor sins.
-   Men and women are assigned their own portions of what they earn; do not envy what Allah gave others.


## Page 84 (4:34–37)
-   Men are the protectors/maintainers of women based on the strengths Allah gave each.
-   Instructions for maintaining family harmony and steps to take if a marriage is failing.
-   The call for reconciliation through arbitrators from both families.
-   The comprehensive command to worship Allah alone and be kind to parents, kin, and neighbors.
-   Condemnation of pride, arrogance, and stinginess in spending wealth.


## Page 85 (4:38–44)
-   Warning against those who spend wealth only to show off and who believe in Satan.
-   Allah is never unjust, even by the weight of an atom; He multiplies good deeds.
